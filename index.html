About

Game development is still the root of how I think.

I started by wanting to build games, and that still shapes my approach to every project. I care about responsiveness, readability, interaction feel, and whether a system gives people a reason to care once they are inside it.

What changed is the range of problems I want to solve with that mindset. XR pulled me deeper into embodiment, presence, and spatial interaction. Research engineering pushed me toward measurable systems, instrumentation, and adaptive logic. Production systems work strengthened how I think about networking, observability, and reliability under real constraints.

The throughline is still the same: I want to build software that reacts to people in meaningful ways. That is why the intersection of games, XR, intelligent interaction, and real-time engineering feels like the right place for me.

01

Design for response.

Whether it is gameplay, XR, or tooling, the system should react clearly and immediately to what the user is doing.

02

Make complexity feel human.

I like work that translates dense technical systems into experiences people can understand, trust, and use.

03

Stay technical without losing identity.

I am most interested in roles where strong engineering and strong interaction thinking both need to be present.
